Consumer Products · 2016–2018

Belkin

Reimagined the customer journey for Belkin’s accessory portfolio—from discovery to device onboarding and support.

Engagement snapshot

  • Role: Lead product manager, connected experiences
  • Team: Multi-disciplinary crew across industrial design, retail marketing, firmware, and mobile apps
  • Channel: Direct-to-consumer ecommerce plus retail partners (Apple, Best Buy, Target)

What we set out to solve

Belkin shipped dozens of accessories yearly. Each SKU had its own onboarding and messaging, leading to churn and missed cross-sell opportunities. We needed a unified story that highlighted ecosystem value without sacrificing simplicity.

Key initiatives

  • Designed a modular setup framework for USB-C hubs, charging solutions, and docks that reused core flows across iOS, Android, and web.
  • Built a content system that tailored help articles, accessories, and promotions based on the products registered by each customer.
  • Partnered with channel marketing to pilot QR-enabled packaging that launched localized onboarding experiences.
  • Integrated support tooling so agents saw the same telemetry, firmware version, and warranty status customers did.

Impact

  • Lifted product registration by 38%, unlocking direct communication with customers outside retail channels.
  • Reduced setup-related returns by 24% through clearer guidance and proactive firmware updates.
  • Enabled a unified accessory recommendation engine responsible for a double-digit attach-rate increase online.

Takeaway

Belkin taught me how to choreograph hardware, packaging, and digital touchpoints. The playbook of modular onboarding and lifecycle personalization now informs my smart home and IoT work.
