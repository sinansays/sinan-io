UMSI Site Redesign Concept

Quick Snapshot

  • Role: UX designer
  • Team: Independent concept project
  • Customers: Prospective students, alumni, and donors exploring the School of Information

Mandate / Opportunity / Problem Scope

The University of Michigan School of Information’s homepage needed a bolder identity that celebrated its people and surfaced calls-to-action. I developed a concept emphasizing storytelling, clarity, and emotional resonance.

What I Led / Delivered / Highlights

  • Designed a minimalist broken-grid layout featuring black-and-white photography, U-M color accents, and people-centric imagery.
  • Structured navigation into dual rows to separate primary journeys from giving and secondary links.
  • Highlighted strengths, news, and events through modular boxes and social proof near the footer.
  • Documented the visual system and interaction guidelines for potential implementation.

Impact / Lasting Value / Takeaway

The concept demonstrated how a refreshed visual and information architecture could align the school’s brand with its community-centric mission.
