Thomson Reuters Onvio Usability Evaluation

Partnered with Thomson Reuters to audit and improve the onboarding experience for their cloud tax platform.

Scope

Onvio was shifting tax professionals from desktop software to the cloud. Adoption lagged because the first-run experience was dense and compliance-critical tasks felt risky.

Approach

  • Conducted remote and in-person usability sessions with CPAs, bookkeepers, and firm admins.
  • Mapped the onboarding journey, documenting 30+ friction points from invite acceptance to first e-file.
  • Collaborated with product and legal teams to prioritize fixes that maintained compliance while reducing cognitive load.

Key recommendations

  • Introduce contextual setup checklists that surfaced only the next best action.
  • Clarify role-based permissions and make audit logs discoverable to ease regulatory concerns.
  • Provide progressive disclosure for advanced workflows (bulk import, firm templates) instead of dumping users into complex modals.

Outcome

The team implemented the prioritized fixes over two releases, leading to a 17% lift in successful onboarding and a marked drop in support escalations during peak tax season.
