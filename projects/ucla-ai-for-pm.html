AI Education · 2023–present

UCLA AI for PM Course

Architected UCLA Extension’s first applied AI product management program, blending research labs with operator-ready playbooks.

Engagement snapshot

  • Role: Program architect, lead instructor, and curriculum author
  • Format: 12-week hybrid cohort with weekly lab sessions and async briefs
  • Partners: UCLA Extension, enterprise guest lecturers (Google, ServiceNow, Amplitude)

What I built

  • Designed a three-track syllabus covering responsible AI, product discovery, and go-to-market enablement with hands-on deliverables each week.
  • Developed lab exercises where students evaluated foundation models, tuned prompts, and instrumented feedback loops using open-source tooling.
  • Produced case studies from my GenCog and Revenue.io work to ground abstract ML concepts in business outcomes.
  • Coached teams through capstone projects, from opportunity framing to stakeholder-ready roadmaps and technical architecture sketches.

Impact

  • Graduated 120+ PMs and operators across three cohorts, with 92% reporting they shipped an AI initiative within six months.
  • Course NPS held at 74, driven by pragmatic tooling walkthroughs and real-world stakeholder management lessons.
  • Adopted as a reference curriculum by internal enablement teams at two Fortune 500 partners.

Why it matters

Organizations are eager to experiment with generative AI but lack a common language between product, design, and engineering. Building this program forced me to codify repeatable patterns for responsible experimentation—playbooks I now reuse when advising enterprise teams.
