Autonomous Vehicles Law & Policy Research

Studied emerging AV legislation to understand how legal frameworks could accelerate—or hinder—responsible deployment.

Context

Before most AV pilots hit public roads, states were writing the first rules. Working with a UCLA research group, I mapped proposed legislation, insurance considerations, and ethical debates to anticipate how product teams would have to adapt.

What I did

  • Reviewed bills across California, Nevada, and federal agencies, cataloging definitions, certification requirements, and liability regimes.
  • Interviewed policymakers, transportation experts, and AV startups to surface the tension between innovation and public trust.
  • Produced briefing memos and scenario planning workshops for civic leaders and product teams evaluating AV roadmaps.

Insights

  • Policy timelines rarely match technology hype cycles; companies need adaptable compliance strategies.
  • Transparency and shared data standards were non-negotiable for regulators long before “responsible AI” entered mainstream vocabulary.
  • Multi-stakeholder collaboration—cities, insurers, OEMs—proved critical to keep deployments aligned with public interest.

Outcome

The research informed guidance adopted by UCLA Law and inspired how I now approach emerging-tech governance. It seeded my enduring focus on aligning product execution with policy and ethics.
