Exploration · 2020–2022

Personal AI

Prototyped a personal knowledge layer that remembers conversations, drafts follow-ups, and keeps decisions transparent.

Engagement snapshot

  • Role: Product strategist and prototyper
  • Collaborators: Independent researchers, privacy engineers, and beta testers across three continents
  • Focus: Ambient capture, consent, and retrieval experiences for individuals and small teams

Hypothesis

Busy operators juggle dozens of micro-commitments daily. Existing productivity tools capture tasks, but not the context behind decisions. A trustworthy personal AI should feel like an extension of memory—private by default, explainable on demand.

What I explored

  • Built a local-first "memory graph" that ingests calendar events, notes, and voice snippets, keeping raw data on-device.
  • Experimented with retrieval-augmented prompts that cite the source artifact before generating suggestions.
  • Designed ambient UI patterns (subtle notification banners, inbox digests, retrospective timelines) to prevent overwhelm.
  • Ran 40+ research sessions with founders, PMs, and caregivers to stress-test consent controls and sharing flows.

Outcomes

  • Alpha users reported saving ~6 hours per week on follow-ups and prep by trusting the memory graph summaries.
  • Informed my approach to provenance: every suggestion links back to the original message, note, or meeting clip.
  • Generated IP (consent ledger patterns, semantic compression techniques) later applied in enterprise copilots.

Where it’s heading

While the product remains in exploration, the work feeds directly into client engagements that demand personal yet compliant AI assistants. The prototypes are a proving ground for balancing delight with privacy.
