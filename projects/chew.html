Personal Project · 2014

Personal Informatics Prototype: Chew

A calm-food tracker that focused on patterns and emotions instead of calorie counting.

Why I built it

Most nutrition apps reduce meals to numbers. Chew explored a different approach: capture context, mood, and intent so users could reflect without judgment.

What I made

  • Designed a lightweight logging flow using emoji and quick swipes to capture mood, social context, and satiety.
  • Created timeline and "pattern cards" that highlighted energy, focus, and sleep correlations over time.
  • Experimented with ambient nudges—gentle reminders after long gaps, weekly rituals for planning, and gratitude prompts.

What I learned

  • Behavior change happens when tools feel like companions, not compliance monitors.
  • Qualitative cues (photos, mood) often spark more reflection than granular macro tracking.
  • This work foreshadowed my later interest in personal AI and context-aware assistants.

Status

Chew remained an experiment but influenced how I design feedback loops in consumer products—especially when habits and wellbeing are involved.
